## 1. General Provisions

### 1.1. About the Logistics Function

The Logistics Department of **AS MyasProm Kombinats** manages the movement of raw materials, packaging, semi-finished goods, and finished meat products across the full supply chain: supplier intake, internal plant transfers, cold storage, national distribution, and export to EU/EEA customers.

The plant ships **more than 1,000 SKUs** and processes approximately **180 inbound deliveries** and **95 outbound shipments** per week during normal season (excluding Q4 peak).

**Logistics headquarters:** Jelgava production site, Building L, rooms L101–L118  
**Registered office:** Riga, Latvia

### 1.2. Purpose of This Policy

This Policy establishes unified rules for:

- inbound logistics and supplier delivery windows;
- warehouse zoning, storage temperatures, and FIFO/FEFO;
- internal material handling and cross-dock operations;
- refrigerated fleet management and route planning;
- outbound distribution, palletisation, and load securing;
- cold-chain monitoring, deviation handling, and traceability;
- customs/export documentation for EU trade;
- KPIs, audits, and contractor compliance.

Mandatory reading for all logistics staff, warehouse operators, drivers, dispatchers, and procurement staff with delivery coordination duties.

### 1.3. Legal and Regulatory Framework

This Policy must be read together with:

- **EU Regulation (EC) No 852/2004** on food hygiene;
- **EU Regulation (EC) No 853/2004** on products of animal origin;
- **EU Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011** on food information (labelling and traceability);
- **Latvian Food Veterinary Service (PVD)** requirements for transport of animal-origin products;
- **ADR** provisions where applicable for limited quantities of cleaning agents and lubricants;
- internal **HACCP** plans and customer-specific quality agreements.

Where this Policy exceeds legal minimums, the stricter requirement applies.

### 1.4. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Chilled lane** | Transport and storage at **0°C to +4°C** for fresh meat and short-shelf-life products |
| **Frozen lane** | Transport and storage at **−18°C or below** |
| **Ambient lane** | Dry goods, packaging, spices — **+15°C to +25°C**, humidity-controlled where specified |
| **Cross-dock** | Direct transfer from inbound dock to outbound without long-term storage |
| **FEFO** | First Expired, First Out — mandatory for all dated stock |
| **ATP certificate** | EU certificate for refrigerated transport equipment |
| **POD** | Proof of Delivery — signed receipt or electronic confirmation |
| **3PL** | Third-party logistics provider under contract |

## 3. Inbound Logistics

### 3.1. Supplier Delivery Windows

All suppliers must book a **mandatory delivery slot** via the supplier portal at least **48 hours** before arrival. Walk-in deliveries without a slot are accepted only with prior written approval from the Inbound Supervisor.

Standard receiving windows by material type:

| Material type | Dock | Window |
|---------------|------|--------|
| Livestock trailers | Dock A1–A4 | 04:00–10:00 daily |
| Fresh meat (boning-in) | Dock B1–B3 | 06:00–14:00 Mon–Sat |
| Frozen blocks / trim | Dock C1–C2 | 08:00–18:00 Mon–Sat |
| Packaging, spices, dry goods | Dock D1–D4 | 08:00–16:00 Mon–Fri |
| Chemicals / sanitation (ADR) | Dock E1 | Tue/Thu 09:00–12:00 only |

Late arrivals beyond **30 minutes** of the booked slot may be rescheduled to the next available window unless the load is classified **critical** (production stoppage risk).

### 3.2. Livestock Intake

Livestock trailers must:

- arrive with valid veterinary transport documents;
- pass vehicle hygiene inspection at the quarantine lane;
- remain within **maximum 2-hour** waiting time before unloading (animal welfare protocol);
- use only approved ramps and anti-slip flooring.

Drivers must remain in the designated waiting area; access to production areas is prohibited.

### 3.3. Raw Material Acceptance

Upon arrival, the receiver checks:

1. **Delivery note** vs purchase order;
2. **Temperature printout** for chilled/frozen goods (mandatory for lanes B and C);
3. **Batch/lot numbers** and use-by dates;
4. **Packaging integrity** — no tears, ice burn, or contamination;
5. **Pallet condition** — no broken boards, protruding nails, or chemical odour.

Rejected loads are quarantined in **Zone Q** (see Section 5.2). Photographic evidence and a non-conformance report (NCR) are mandatory.

### 3.4. Unloading and Put-Away SLA

| Lane | Target unload-to-rack time |
|------|---------------------------|
| Chilled raw | **45 minutes** |
| Frozen raw | **60 minutes** |
| Ambient packaging | **90 minutes** |

Put-away must respect **FEFO** and zone temperature limits. Mixed pallets are prohibited in chilled and frozen zones.

Organic-certified raw material segregation rules are defined in **Section 16.3**.

## 4. Warehouse Management

### 4.1. Warehouse Zones

| Zone | Code | Temperature | Contents |
|------|------|-------------|----------|
| Raw chilled | RC-01 | 0°C to +2°C | Fresh pork, beef, poultry prior to processing |
| Raw frozen | RF-01 | −22°C to −18°C | Frozen blocks, trim, imported cuts |
| WIP chilled | WIP-01 | 0°C to +4°C | Semi-finished between production stages |
| Finished chilled | FC-01 | 0°C to +4°C | Packaged fresh sausages, mince, cuts |
| Finished frozen | FF-01 | −24°C to −18°C | Frozen finished goods |
| Ambient | AM-01 | +15°C to +25°C | Packaging film, casings, labels, dry spices |
| Quarantine | Q-01 | As per source lane | Holds pending QA release or NCR |
| Export staging | EX-01 | Lane-specific | Pallet build for export orders |

### 4.2. Quarantine and Release

No stock moves from **Zone Q** to production or dispatch without **QA release** signed in the WMS. Maximum quarantine hold without QA decision: **72 hours** (chilled) / **7 days** (frozen), after which disposal or return-to-supplier is initiated.

### 4.3. Inventory Accuracy

- **Cycle counts:** high-velocity SKUs weekly; full zone count monthly;
- **Acceptable inventory accuracy:** **≥ 98.5%** by line count;
- Discrepancies above **EUR 500** value trigger a root-cause investigation within **5 business days**.

### 4.4. Pallet and Racking Standards

- **EUR pallet** (1200 × 800 mm) is standard; **UK pallet** (1200 × 1000 mm) allowed only in export staging with customer approval;
- Maximum rack height: **4 levels** in frozen, **5 levels** in ambient;
- Chilled/frozen pallets: **max 1,000 kg** gross weight unless racking engineer approval on file;
- Stretch-wrap: minimum **4 revolutions** at base, **2** at top; red **"TOP"** labels on unstable loads.

Damaged pallet replacement fees for supplier-caused damage are listed in **Appendix F**.

### 4.5. Internal Transfers

Forklift transfers between zones follow one-way hygiene flow: **raw → WIP → finished**. Reverse movement requires sanitation SOP and QA sign-off.

Night-shift internal transfer authorisation limits are defined in **Section 17.1**.

## 5. Cold Chain Management

### 5.1. Temperature Monitoring

All chilled and frozen storage rooms have **continuous IoT sensors** logging at **5-minute** intervals. Refrigerated trucks have **dual probes** (cargo + return air) with GPS-linked loggers.

Data retention: **3 years** minimum for audit and customer traceability.

### 5.2. Acceptable Ranges in Transit

| Product lane | Acceptable range during transport |
|--------------|-----------------------------------|
| Chilled fresh meat | **0°C to +4°C** (brief spikes to +6°C for **≤ 20 minutes** tolerated with CAPA) |
| Frozen products | **≤ −15°C** at delivery (target −18°C) |
| Deep frozen export | **≤ −18°C** at all times |

### 5.3. Deviation Handling

If temperature exceeds limits:

1. Driver or warehouse alerts Cold Chain desk (**ext. 2419**);
2. Load is marked **"TEMP DEVIATION"** in WMS;
3. QA assesses safety within **2 hours** for chilled / **4 hours** for frozen;
4. Disposition: release, rework (if allowed), donate (charity protocol), or destroy.

Repeated deviations from the same carrier trigger review per **Section 14.2**.

### 5.4. Pre-Trip Inspection (PTI)

Every refrigerated vehicle undergoes **PTI** before loading:

- refrigeration unit run test **≥ 30 minutes**;
- calibration sticker valid;
- interior hygiene check;
- drain plugs sealed;
- ATP certificate valid (where required).

Failed PTI — vehicle must not be loaded. Backup vehicle SLA is defined in **Appendix E**.

### 6.2. Driver Requirements

- Category **C/C+E** licence as applicable;
- ADR awareness for sanitation chemical returns;
- Food transport hygiene training — **annual** refresher;
- Medical fitness per national commercial driver rules;
- No alcohol: **0.0‰** at pre-trip breath test on site.

### 6.3. Route Planning

Routes are planned daily by the TMS (Transport Management System) with:

- **maximum 9 hours** driving time per driver (EU drivers' hours rules);
- **mandatory 45-minute** break after 4.5 hours driving;
- chilled deliveries to retail: **door-to-door ≤ 12 hours** from dispatch;
- frozen export to Klaipėda port: **≤ 18 hours** transit target.

Fuel surcharge adjustments for contracted carriers are defined in **Appendix C**.

### 6.4. Vehicle Hygiene Between Loads

- **Full wash-down** after raw meat or allergen-containing loads;
- **Sanitisation certificate** in cab folder for audit;
- **Minimum 15-minute** airing between chilled product types when no wash bay available (documented exception only).

Allergen changeover deep-clean requirements are defined in **Section 16.2**.

## 7. Outbound Distribution

### 7.1. Order Cut-Off Times

| Customer channel | Order cut-off | Dispatch same day |
|------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| National retail chains | 14:00 previous day | Yes, if slot confirmed |
| HoReCa (Riga) | 10:00 same day | Yes, afternoon route |
| Export EU | 12:00 **2 business days** before | Per sailing / border slot |
| E-commerce B2B | 16:00 previous day | Next business day |

### 7.2. Picking and Packing

- Pick lists generated **FEFO**-sorted;
- **Double-check** at packing station for retail chains (barcode scan);
- **Ice packs** or **dry ice** per customer spec for parcels > 4 hours transit;
- **Health mark** and lot code visible on outer case.

### 7.3. Load Securing

- Pallet loads secured with straps or load bars;
- **No mixed lanes** on the same vehicle unless partition curtain and QA approval;
- Temperature logger placed on **top middle pallet** — position recorded on CMR/delivery note.

### 7.4. Proof of Delivery

POD must include:

- recipient name and signature (or ePOD timestamp);
- arrival temperature (chilled/frozen);
- exception codes for shortages or damage.

Missing POD within **48 hours** blocks invoice release for that shipment.

Retail chain OTIF penalty thresholds are defined in **Section 19.1**.

## 11. Traceability and Recall

### 11.1. Lot Traceability

Every pallet carries WMS label: **SKU, lot, best-before, quantity, zone, timestamp**.

Forward and backward trace must be achievable within **4 hours** for any lot (regulatory and customer audit requirement).

### 11.2. Mock Recall

Logistics participates in **annual mock recall** drill. Target: identify all affected pallets in transit and in DC within **2 hours**.

### 11.3. Product Withdrawal Transport

Withdrawn stock is transported in **sealed, labelled** vehicles to destruction or rework facilities. Chain of custody log mandatory.

## 12. Health, Safety, and Site Rules

### 12.1. Warehouse Traffic

- Forklift speed **≤ 10 km/h** indoors;
- Pedestrian walkways marked — no shortcuts through rack aisles;
- High-visibility vest mandatory in dock and yard areas.

### 12.2. Loading Dock Safety

- Vehicle wheels chocked during loading;
- Dock leveller capacity checked before heavy livestock equipment;
- **No mobile phone use** while operating MHE (material handling equipment).

### 12.3. PPE in Logistics Areas

Steel-toe boots, gloves for load handling, hearing protection in yard during peak compressor hours. Sanitary zone PPE rules apply when entering WIP/FC through production airlocks.

## 16. Special Product Handling

### 16.1. Halal and Kosher Segregation

Certified halal/kosher lines use dedicated **purple-labelled** pallets, segregated staging in **EX-02**, and approved carriers only. Cross-contamination is a **critical non-conformance**.

### 16.2. Allergen Changeover

After loads containing **mustard, milk powder, or celery** allergen declarations, vehicles require **full wash-down** before unrelated retail loads. The minimum 15-minute airing shortcut in Section 6.4 does **not** apply.

### 16.3. Organic Raw Material Segregation

Organic-certified raw meat must be received on **cleaned or disposable** pallets, stored in **RC-ORG** (physically separated bay in RC-01), and booked in WMS with organic flag. Co-mingling with conventional raw in the same rack bay is prohibited.

## 17. Night Operations and Peak Season

### 17.1. Night-Shift Internal Transfer Limits

Between **22:00 and 06:00**, forklift transfers from **raw zones to WIP** require **Shift Supervisor + QA on-call** approval. Maximum **6 pallet moves** per night unless COO emergency order.

### 17.2. Q4 Peak (October–December)

During Q4 peak, outbound volume may increase **up to 35%** above baseline. Temporary agency drivers allowed after **2-hour** site induction. Extra chilled capacity may be leased — ATP and insurance verification mandatory.

Q4 inbound dock extension hours are defined in **Appendix D**.

## 18. Incidents and Business Continuity

### 18.1. Major Disruption Events

Fire, power loss in frozen storage, or refrigeration plant failure triggers **Business Continuity Plan BCP-LOG-01**:

- generator priority to FF-01 and RF-01;
- emergency dry ice procurement authorised up to **EUR 15,000** per incident by COO;
- customer notification within **2 hours** for affected outbound orders.

### 18.2. After-Hours Cold Chain Escalation

| Step | Contact | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Dispatcher ext. 2419 | Log deviation, instruct driver |
| 2 | QA on-call | Safety disposition |
| 3 | Logistics Manager mobile | Carrier backup / reroute |
| 4 | COO | Customer and regulatory notification if public health risk |

## 19. Customer Service Levels and Penalties

### 19.1. Retail OTIF Penalty Threshold

For **Rimi** and **Maxima** national DC deliveries, OTIF below **94%** in a calendar month triggers a **0.8%** credit on affected line invoices. Root-cause report due within **7 business days**.

Standard OTIF target for these accounts remains **96%** as per Section 14.1 unless a separate contract states otherwise.

### 19.2. Temperature Rejection at Customer

If customer rejects a load for documented temperature breach attributable to MyasProm logistics, logistics bears return transport and disposal cost. Carrier recovery pursued if third-party fault is proven.

### Appendix C. Carrier Fuel Surcharge and Rebate Table

Base contract rates are adjusted monthly using the **Latvian diesel index (LV DIESEL IDX)**.

- Surcharge applies when index rises **> 5%** above contract baseline — **+1.2%** of linehaul per additional **2%** index increase (cap **+8%**);
- Rebate applies when index falls **> 5%** below baseline — **−0.6%** per **2%** decrease (cap **−4%**);
- **Dedicated carrier rebate:** carriers with **≥ 40 loads/month** receive an additional **−1.5%** on chilled national lanes if OTD **≥ 97%** for the quarter.

### Appendix G. RPC Loss Charge Waiver Conditions

The standard **EUR 12** RPC replacement charge (Section 9.1) is **waived** when **all** of the following are proven:

1. Police or carrier incident report filed within **24 hours**;
2. GPS/TMS shows vehicle deviation or unauthorised stop;
3. QA confirms no employee negligence in crate handling.

Partial waiver (**50%**) applies if only conditions 1 and 3 are met.
