## 1. General Provisions

### 1.1. About the Company

AS **MyasProm Kombinats** is a large-scale meat processing enterprise operating a full production cycle: livestock and poultry intake, slaughter, cutting, thermal and cold processing, semi-finished products, sausages, delicatessen, and packaged goods. The product catalogue includes **more than 1,000 SKUs** of meat and meat-containing products.

The plant operates in **continuous production mode** (3 shifts, 7 days per week) in compliance with EU food safety regulations, **HACCP**, veterinary and sanitary requirements, and internal quality standards.

**Registered office:** Riga, Latvia  
**Production site:** Jelgava Municipality, Latvia

### 1.2. Purpose of This Policy

This Policy establishes unified rules and procedures for:

- recruitment, hiring, and onboarding;
- employment relations, work schedules, and remuneration;
- leave, sick absence, and other types of time off;
- disciplinary measures and termination of employment;
- occupational safety, hygiene, and conduct on the production floor;
- training, certification, and internal career development.

The Policy is mandatory reading for all employees upon hire and annually during safety and compliance briefings.

### 1.3. Legal Framework

This Policy is based on and must be read together with:

- **Latvian Labour Law** (*Darba likums*);
- **Law on Holidays** (*Atvaļinājuma likums*);
- **Law on Mandatory State Social Insurance**;
- **EU Regulation (EC) No 852/2004** on food hygiene;
- applicable collective agreements and individual employment contracts.

Where this Policy provides benefits **above** the legal minimum, the more favourable term applies to the employee.

### 1.4. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Production staff** | Workers in slaughter, cutting, thermal processing, sausage, packaging, cold storage, and warehouse zones |
| **Support staff** | Technologists, lab technicians, veterinarians, QA, logistics, mechanics, sanitation workers |
| **Office staff** | Administration, HR, finance, sales, procurement, IT |
| **Sanitary zone** | Areas with heightened hygiene, PPE, and access-control requirements |
| **Probation period** | Period for assessing employee suitability for the role |
| **PPE** | Personal protective equipment (coats, aprons, gloves, hairnets, overshoes, helmets) |

## 3. Recruitment and Hiring

### 3.1. Workforce Planning

Headcount demand is prepared:

1. By department heads — quarterly;
2. By the Production Director — consolidated plan;
3. By HR — vacancy publication and hiring budget.

Seasonal peaks (pre-Christmas period, harvest campaigns) may allow **temporary headcount expansion of up to 15%** above the approved staffing level by CEO order.

### 3.2. Vacancy Categories

| Category | Example roles | Main requirements |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| A — safety-critical | Slaughter line operator, veterinarian, HACCP technologist | Relevant education, health book, min. 1 year experience |
| B — production | Cutter, packer, thermal chamber operator | Health book, trainability, OSH clearance |
| C — support | Storekeeper, loader, sanitation worker | Health book, briefing completion |
| D — office | Sales manager, accountant, HR specialist | Higher or vocational education |

### 3.3. Standard Hiring Process

1. **Application** — via website, agency, or internal referral.
2. **CV screening** — up to 5 business days.
3. **Phone interview** — HR.
4. **In-person/video interview** — line manager + HR.
5. **Skills assessment** — for categories A and B (practical task or hygiene knowledge test).
6. **Medical examination** — mandatory before production access (per Latvian food-industry requirements).
7. **Document check** — passport/ID, tax number, employment history, diploma, occupational health certificate.
8. **Offer letter** — role, salary, start date, probation period.
9. **Onboarding** — employment contract, policy acknowledgements, access badge issuance.

**Typical hiring cycle:** 10–21 business days (category D), 7–14 days (category B during labour shortages).

### 3.4. Employee Referral Programme

An employee who successfully refers a candidate receives a **one-time bonus of EUR 300**, provided the referred person:

- is hired on a permanent contract;
- completes **3 months** of employment;
- receives no disciplinary sanctions during that period.

### 3.5. Grounds for Refusal to Hire

Employment **must be declined** if the candidate:

- lacks a valid occupational health certificate (production roles);
- tests positive for alcohol/narcotics at pre-employment screening;
- fails the sanitary briefing on first attempt (re-test allowed after **14 days**);
- conceals disqualifying convictions for roles with financial responsibility (case-by-case legal review).

### 4.2. Onboarding Programme

| Period | Activities |
|--------|------------|
| Day 1 | General OSH briefing, veterinary/sanitary briefing, PPE issue, plant tour |
| Week 1 | Workplace mentor, HACCP training for assigned zone |
| Month 1 | Interim assessment by shift supervisor |
| Month 3 | Final probation review |

A **mentor** is appointed by department order. Mentors receive an allowance of **EUR 60/month** per mentee (maximum 2 mentees at a time).

### 4.3. Probation Period

Under Latvian Labour Law, probation may not exceed **3 months** unless a longer period is expressly agreed for managers (up to **6 months** for executive roles by written agreement).

| Role category | Company standard probation |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| Production workers (B, C) | **1 month** |
| Specialists, supervisors, technologists (A) | **3 months** |
| Middle management | **3 months** |
| Executive management | **up to 6 months** (separate agreement) |

During probation:

- annual leave is granted on **general terms** if length of service allows;
- employer-initiated termination notice — **3 days** minimum (or per contract);
- employee-initiated resignation notice — **3 days** minimum during probation.

## 5. Working Time and Shift Patterns

### 5.1. Shifts

Standard production schedule:

- **Shift 1:** 06:00–14:00  
- **Shift 2:** 14:00–22:00  
- **Shift 3:** 22:00–06:00  

Administration: Monday–Friday, 08:00–17:00, lunch 12:00–13:00.

### 5.2. Summarised Working Time Accounting

Production staff use **summarised working-time accounting** with a **quarterly** reference period. Hours are aligned with the official working-time calendar of Latvia.

### 5.3. Overtime

Overtime is permitted only with employee consent (except emergencies). Pay:

- first 2 hours — **×1.5**;
- subsequent hours — **×2**;
- work on weekly rest day or public holiday — **×2** plus compensatory rest day (employee choice where law allows).

**Annual overtime cap:** no more than **144 hours** per employee (stricter internal limit; legal maximum monitored by HR).

### 5.4. Night Work

Work between 22:00 and 06:00 receives a **20%** supplement on the hourly rate (in addition to any shift premium).

### 5.5. Breaks

- 8-hour shift — **40 minutes** break (company canteen; subsidised hot meal for shift workers).
- 12-hour shift — two breaks: **30 + 40 minutes**.

## 7. Leave and Planned Absence

### 7.1. Annual Paid Leave

**Legal minimum in Latvia:** **4 calendar weeks** (20 working days if the employee works a standard 5-day week).

**Company baseline:** **28 calendar days** per year for all employees.

Additional leave days:

| Basis | Extra days |
|-------|------------|
| Harmful working conditions (slaughter, cutting) | **+7 calendar days** |
| Permanent work in cold chambers | **+5 calendar days** |
| Managers with irregular working hours | **+3 calendar days** |
| Disability group I–II (if declared and documented) | **+2 calendar days** |

**Example maximum** for a cutter in a cold zone in slaughter: 28 + 7 + 5 = **40 calendar days** (when multiple grounds apply per internal rules).

### 7.2. Right to Leave

The right to the first annual leave arises after **6 months** of continuous employment. By mutual agreement, leave may be granted earlier.

### 7.3. Leave Schedule

- Annual leave schedule approved by **15 December** for the following year.
- Leave request submitted to HR **at least 14 calendar days** before start date.
- Postponement — by agreement with manager; for operational reasons — CEO order with **7 days** notice to employee.

### 7.4. Splitting Leave

Leave may be split into parts. **At least one part must be at least 2 consecutive calendar weeks** (Latvian legal requirement).

### 7.5. Unpaid Leave

Granted on employee request:

- up to **14 calendar days** per year — line manager approval;
- beyond 14 days — HR Director approval.

Employees with **5+ years** at the company: up to **30 calendar days** unpaid leave per year with HR Director approval.

### 7.6. Study Leave

Employees in accredited full-time programmes aligned with their role may receive paid study leave per employer confirmation and collective agreement (up to **15 working days** per academic year).

## 8. Sick Leave and Temporary Incapacity

### 8.1. Procedure

When ill, the employee must:

1. Notify the line manager **before shift start** (call/SMS/messenger).
2. Visit a doctor and obtain an **e-sick leave certificate** (Latvian SSIA system).
3. Inform HR of the certificate number **within 1 business day** of opening sick leave.

### 8.2. Pay

Sickness benefit is paid per **State Social Insurance Agency (SSIA)** rules depending on insurance record. The employer may top up to **80%** of average earnings for employees with **3+ years** at the company (per collective agreement).

### 8.3. Controls

HR may request an **unscheduled medical examination** if abuse is suspected (no more than once per 12 months, in compliance with law).

## 11. Discipline and Sanctions

### 11.1. Types of Violations

**Minor:** lateness up to 30 minutes, dress code breach outside sanitary zone.  
**Moderate:** lateness over 30 minutes, unexcused absence, hygiene breach in sanitary zone, refusal to wear PPE.  
**Serious:** intoxication at work, smoking in production areas, HACCP sabotage, recipe disclosure, product theft.

### 11.2. Disciplinary Measures

1. Verbal warning (documented)  
2. Written reprimand  
3. Termination per **Section 101** of the Latvian Labour Law and employment contract  

Disciplinary action must generally be applied **within 1 month** of discovery and **within 6 months** of the violation (unless law provides otherwise).

### 11.3. Food Production Specific Rules

- **First** sanitary-zone violation — written reprimand + unscheduled briefing.  
- **Second** within 12 months — termination for breach of employment duties / loss of trust (legal review required).  
- **Foreign object contamination** attributable to employee negligence — commission review, reprimand or termination.

## 12. Termination of Employment

### 12.1. Resignation by Employee

Written notice required:

| Category | Notice period |
|----------|---------------|
| During probation | **3 days** (or per contract) |
| Standard employees | **1 month** (Latvian Labour Law default) |
| Managers / chief accountant | **1 month** (or longer if contract states) |

On last working day: employment certificate, social insurance documents, final payroll settlement.

### 12.2. Termination by Employer

Grounds per **Latvian Labour Law** (including redundancy, misconduct, incapacity). Additional company grounds:

- repeated HACCP violations;
- two disciplinary sanctions within 12 months;
- loss of occupational health certificate for **more than 30 days**.

**Redundancy (collective or individual):** notice **1 month** in advance (or pay in lieu where legally permitted); consultation with employee representatives if applicable; notification to State Employment Agency when required.

### 12.3. Severance on Redundancy

- Statutory severance per **Latvian Labour Law** based on tenure;
- Company supplement: up to **1 month’s salary** for tenure **10+ years** (collective agreement).

### 12.4. Mutual Termination Agreement

Allowed with agreed compensation (typically **1–3 months’ salary**). Must be in writing.

### 12.5. Clearance Checklist

Upon exit:

- return PPE and access badge;
- return tools and keys;
- veterinary service sign-off (production);
- clear canteen/library debts.

## 15. Occupational Safety, Hygiene, and PPE

### 15.1. Medical Examinations

- Pre-employment — before hire;
- Periodic — **every 12 months** (production), **every 24 months** (office);
- Extraordinary — when transferring to sanitary zone or after sick leave **>60 days**.

### 15.2. PPE

Issued free per norms. Employee may be charged for **negligent loss** of PPE — up to **30%** of monthly salary (documented act).

### 15.3. Personal Hygiene in Sanitary Zones

Mandatory: hand washing (min. 20 sec), disinfection, change shoes, hair covering, no jewellery or watches on the production floor.
